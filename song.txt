**“The Kingdom Was Open”**
Song: https://youtu.be/5QU0yP-RjqU

**Verse 1**
On the middle cushion of the brown porch throne,
Sat an old dog keeping the pool his own.
White fence shining like a crown in the sun,
Blue water waiting for the kids to come.

The slide stood quiet with a guilty grin,
Like it knew the trouble it had once been in.
And the umbrellas stood with nothing to say,
All dressed up for a summer day.

**Pre-Chorus**
He heard ice in the kitchen,
He smelled bacon in the walls,
He knew every little moment
Before the whole house called.

**Chorus**
Oh, he was the lifeguard,
The king of the shade,
Watching over ripples
That the morning made.

With his paws folded under,
And his heart big as stone,
He guarded the silence
Till the family came home.

And when somebody asked,
“Where’s our boy?” from inside,
He tapped his tail once—
Let the kingdom come alive.

**Verse 2**
No dogs in the water, that was the law,
Though history had seen one enormous paw.
One splash, one shout, one chlorine kiss,
One little voice saying, “He’s a bear like this.”

Now he kept his station, noble and wide,
With the pool blue world spread out at his side.
The glass-top table held another old hound,
But neither one barked, so peace stayed around.

**Pre-Chorus**
He had no grand ambition,
No calendar, no plan,
Just faith in fallen hot dogs
And the kindness of a hand.

**Chorus**
Oh, he was the lifeguard,
The king of the shade,
Watching over ripples
That the morning made.

With his paws folded under,
And his heart big as stone,
He guarded the silence
Till the family came home.

And when somebody asked,
“Where’s our boy?” from inside,
He tapped his tail once—
Let the kingdom come alive.

**Bridge**
Every ordinary afternoon
Has a hinge nobody sees,
A gate, a laugh, a cooler lid,
A warm hand on your knees.

No trumpets for the holy things,
No banners in the air,
Just a dog on outdoor furniture
Pretending not to care.

**Final Chorus**
Oh, he was the lifeguard,
The king of the shade,
Watching over summer
Like it might get away.

With the pool full of sky,
And the slide standing tall,
He waited for the footsteps,
For the voices, for it all.

And when the day came running,
Wet and wild and bright,
He tapped his tail once—
And the world turned right.

**Outro**
On the middle cushion of the brown porch throne,
An old dog smiled without moving a bone.
The water kept laughing, the sunlight shone,
And the kingdom was open—
The kingdom was home.
